Writer's Hub is a writing tool built for fiction authors who want more than a blank page. It combines a distraction-free prose editor with a world-building system — so your characters, locations, and lore live alongside your chapters, not in a separate document you keep forgetting to update.

## The two sides of Writer's Hub

Everything in the app falls into one of two areas:

**The Reader side** is where published stories live. When you publish your work, readers can browse it on the home feed, follow chapters, leave highlights, and track their progress. You access this from the Home and Write links in the top navigation.

**The Editor side** is your private writing workspace. Once you open a project, you get a three-panel layout — a document tree on the left, your writing canvas in the center, and a story map on the right.

## Creating your first project

From the dashboard, click **New project**. Give it a title (your working title — readers won't see this unless you publish it as-is) and an optional description for your own reference.

Every project starts with a single **Canon** branch. Think of Canon as the official timeline of your story. Everything you write here is your primary draft.

## The three-panel editor layout

Once you open a project, you'll see:

**Left panel — Document tree**
This is your outline. Chapters are listed at the top. Below them are your world-building entities: Characters, Locations, Lore, and Objects. Click any item to open it in the editor. Use the **+** button on hover to create new items in each section.

**Center — Writing canvas**
This is where you write. Click a chapter title to open it. The title is editable — click it and type. Your work saves automatically every 1.5 seconds; a small "Saved" indicator appears briefly in the top bar.

**Right panel — Story map**
A live force-directed graph of your story's connections. Chapters are larger dark nodes. Entities are colored by type. Edges appear when you link entities inside chapter text using `[[wikilink]]` syntax. You can toggle this panel open and closed from the top bar.

## Navigating the top bar

The top bar gives you quick access to:

- **Sidebar toggle** — show or hide the left panel
- **Dashboard link** — go back to your project list
- **Branch selector** — switch between story branches (more on this in the branching guide)
- **Save indicator** — confirms your work is saved
- **Right panel toggle** — show or hide the story map

## Keyboard shortcuts

| Shortcut | Action |
|---|---|
| `⌘ .` | Toggle focus mode |
| `⌘ ⇧ S` | Save a named checkpoint |
