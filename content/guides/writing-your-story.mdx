The writing canvas in Writer's Hub is built around one idea: get out of your way. There's no toolbar cluttering the top of the screen, no formatting panels to manage. The interface appears when you need it and hides when you don't.

## The editor basics

Click any chapter in the left panel to open it. The chapter title sits above the prose area — click it to rename it. Your text begins below.

The editor uses **Lora**, a serif typeface chosen for long-form reading and writing. Paragraphs breathe at 1.8 line height. The writing column is capped at 680px — wide enough to feel spacious, narrow enough that your eyes never have to travel too far across the line.

## Autosave

You never need to press save. The editor watches for changes and saves automatically 1.5 seconds after you stop typing. A small **Saved** indicator appears in the top bar and fades out after a moment.

If something goes wrong, the indicator shows **Save failed** in red. This is rare — usually a network hiccup. Your text is still in the editor; wait a moment and it will retry on your next keystroke.

## The floating toolbar

Select any text in the editor and a small toolbar appears above your selection. It gives you:

- **Bold** and **Italic** — basic formatting
- **H1** and **H2** — section headings
- **Blockquote** — for epigraphs, letters, or stylistic breaks
- **Rewrite** (the wand icon) — opens the rewrite surface for the current paragraph

The toolbar disappears when you click away or collapse your selection.

## Focus mode

Press `⌘ .` to enter focus mode. In focus mode, every paragraph except the one your cursor is in fades to low opacity. Only what you're writing right now is fully visible. This is useful when you're revising and don't want the surrounding prose to pull your attention.

Press `⌘ .` again to exit.

## Rewriting paragraphs

Place your cursor anywhere in a paragraph and select some text, then click the **wand icon** in the floating toolbar. The rewrite surface opens with your original paragraph at the top.

You can write up to three alternative versions in the attempt boxes below. Each attempt has a **Compare** toggle — select two versions and click **Compare** to open a side-by-side view. The two versions appear as clean prose columns. Read both. Pick the one that feels right. The other one isn't deleted — it stays in your paragraph history.

Click **Use this** under either version to replace the paragraph in your editor.

## Paragraph history

Every paragraph has a version history. When you've rewritten a paragraph at least once, the wand icon in the toolbar opens the **History drawer** instead of the rewrite surface. The drawer shows every saved version, oldest to newest, with timestamps.

Click **Restore this version** to bring an old version back. The current version is marked so you always know where you are.

## Checkpoints

A checkpoint is a named snapshot of your entire chapter at a specific moment. Think of it like a commit in version control — you're saying "the chapter is in a good state right now, remember this."

To save a checkpoint, press `⌘ ⇧ S` or click the **flag icon** in the chapter header. A small modal asks for an optional message — something like "End of Act 1" or "Before the twist". Click **Save checkpoint**.

## The chapter timeline

Click the **clock icon** in the chapter header to open the timeline strip. It shows all your checkpoints as dots on a horizontal line, oldest on the left, newest on the right. A green **Now** dot marks your current state.

Click any checkpoint dot to open a read-only preview of the chapter as it was at that moment. From the preview, you can click **Restore to this version** to roll the chapter back. When you restore, the current state is automatically saved as a new checkpoint first — so you can always get back to where you were.

## Story branching

If you want to explore an alternate direction — a different ending, a subplot that might not work, a total structural overhaul — create a **branch**.

Click the branch name in the top bar (it shows "Canon" by default) to open the branch dropdown. Click **Fork new branch** and give the branch a name like "Dark ending" or "Alt timeline". The app copies your entire Canon — all chapters and entities — to the new branch. Your Canon is untouched.

You can switch between branches from the same dropdown. Each branch has its own document tree, its own checkpoints, and its own story map.

When you're happy with a branch and want it to become your primary draft, select it in the dropdown and click the **star icon** to set it as Canon.
